My ToK exhibition overview

"Convergence verification of the Collatz problem"

A paper on testing all numbers up to 200 quintillion, checking if they comply with the Collatz problem, which suggests that all positive integers reach 1 after following given rules. As an example of poor justification, the paper explores how good justification should either directly or logically show the validity of the entirety of the claim.

Sedna, a dwarf planet

A dwarf planet with a very large orbit, which alongside a clustering of objects similar to Sedna, sparked speculation about a ninth planet as the cause of this distribution. It explores how a well-backed theoretical model alone can count as good justification for the knowledge claim of the existence of Planet Nine.

A350 aircraft wing stress test rig

An experimental setup used to verify that the design of the aircraft wing fits safety margins, justifying the claim that the A350 airliner is safe. The rig explores how having large margins, empirical measurements, and review by authorities ensure the quality of justification.
